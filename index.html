① Day 1 — Land → Alpacas → Tekapo

10:40 local
Arrive Christchurch Airport (CHC)
Christchurch
Land on SQ 297. Clear immigration via crew lane (lanes 16 & 17), collect bags, pick up the rental car. Budget 60–90 min to be road-ready.
~12:00
Sherlin Alpaca Farm
Alpacas West Melton
25 min west of the airport. Hand-feed a big herd, wander the paddocks, reset from the overnight flight before the long drive south.
🦙 On the alpaca question Dedicated alpaca farms in the Mackenzie Basin are rare — it's mostly sheep and cattle country. Sherlin adds only ~30 min to the total journey and is genuinely great. Worth the small detour.
↗ View on Maps
13:30
Lunch — Geraldine
On route
Head south on the inland scenic route. Geraldine is a quiet little town with good cafés and a bakery. ~1.5 hr from Sherlin, ~1 hr from Tekapo.
15:30
Arrive Lake Tekapo village
Tekapo
Check into your Tekapo accommodation (see Tekapo Stay ↓). The turquoise lake is right there — you'll understand the hype immediately.
16:00 – 17:30
Church of the Good Shepherd & lakeshore
Tekapo
Walk to the iconic stone church on the lake edge and stroll the rocky shoreline. Best in the late afternoon light — cold air, big mountains, ridiculous colour.
17:30 – 18:45
Mt John Summit & Astro Café
Tekapo
Drive up for 360° views over the lake and Southern Alps at golden hour. Coffee at Astro Café before it closes. One of the great NZ viewpoints.
19:00
Dinner in the village
Tekapo
Blue Lake Eatery & Bar for hearty Kiwi mains (Mt Cook salmon, lamb shanks), or Kohan for Japanese and something lighter. Both are consistently good.
21:00 – late
Stargazing & hot pools
Dark Sky
Tekapo Star Gazing — guided night-sky intro then float in hot pools under the Aoraki Mackenzie Dark Sky Reserve. Or the Dark Sky Project for a full telescope session. Book ahead.

② Day 2 — Tekapo Morning → Back to Christchurch

08:00
Morning — Peninsula Walkway
Tekapo
Easy 2–3 hr loop around the Tekapo peninsula. Stunning lake views in the morning light before the tour buses arrive. Bring a layer — it's cold in the Mackenzie Basin mornings.
10:30
Brunch — The Greedy Cow
Tekapo
Tekapo's most popular café. Big cabinet food, proper cooked breakfasts, good coffee. Vego/vegan/GF options. Go early — fills up fast.
12:00
Drive back — stop at Rakaia Gorge
On route
Head north via SH8/SH72. Pull over at the Rakaia Gorge lookout — 5 min stop over the milky-blue river. Easy add-on if the weather is clear.
~15:00
Christchurch — Gondola or Botanic Gardens
Christchurch
Gondola for a cable car up to views over the city, Lyttelton Harbour and the Alps. Or Botanic Gardens for a slow afternoon stroll along the Avon River. Pick your mood.
Evening
Check in — Fable Christchurch
Crew Hotel
166 Gloucester Street, Christchurch Central. Your official crew layover hotel — double-bedded room, free gym, 25% off food & drinks. Full details below.
Sunset
Optional — He Puna Taimoana
New Brighton
Modern oceanfront hot pools by the New Brighton pier. Float in hot water as the sun drops over the Pacific. ~20 min from the Fable. Perfect end to the trip.

🍽 Christchurch Food Guide

Raining outside? Sorted. 14 places covering everything from speakeasies to dim sum — filtered by mood. All near Fable Hotel (Gloucester St), or a short Uber away.

☕ Cafes & Brunch
C1 Espresso CBD · 5 min walk

A Christchurch institution inside a converted old Post Office. The gimmick is real: food travels to your table via pneumatic tube at 140 km/h. Great coffee, big all-day menu (eggs benny, bagels, French toast). Perfect rainy-day spot — atmospheric, spacious, and no rush to leave.

📍 150-185 High St (corner Tuam St) ⏰ Mon–Fri 07:00–21:00 · Sat–Sun 07:00–17:00 💵 NZD 10–30pp
Pneumatic tube delivery CHC icon Great for rainy days No booking needed

Child Sister CBD · 6 min walk

Trendy Korean-inspired brunch café with bold, punchy flavours — the most unique brunch in CHC. Signature dishes: Kimchi Rice Omelette, Gochujang Shakshuka, Kimchi Grilled Cheese. Sleek modern interior, excellent coffee, fresh ingredients. If you want Korean flavours for breakfast, this is it.

📍 277 Manchester St, Christchurch CBD ⏰ Daily 08:00–16:00 (kitchen closes 14:30) 💵 NZD 20–30pp 📞 03 222 2117

🍸 Bars & Speakeasies
Austin Club CBD · 10 min walk

CHC's best speakeasy — a prohibition-era cocktail bar hidden in a basement laneway off High Street. Entry by monthly password only (check their Instagram @austinclubchch for the current one). Look for the neon green sign and a pineapple door. The cocktails are genuinely excellent and the room feels like 1920s New York.

📍 236 High Street (laneway beside Glassons) ⏰ Wed–Sat 17:00–late 💵 NZD 15–25 per cocktail 🔑 Password required — check @austinclubchch on Instagram
Speakeasy Password entry 1920s vibe No booking

Red Light District CBD · 12 min walk

CHC's first speakeasy-style bar, hidden behind a laundromat on Victoria Street. Enter down the side alley, through the launderette — the door with the tumble dryer front on it. Inside: moody Shanghai turn-of-the-century atmosphere, red lanterns, excellent cocktails. One of the city's most unique atmospheres.

📍 123 Victoria St (through the laundromat) ⏰ Daily 17:00–midnight 💵 NZD 17–43pp 📞 +64 3-365 0862

OGB (Old Government Building) Cathedral Square · 3 min walk

Closest bar to your hotel — CHC's most beautiful heritage venue, a grand 1920s building right on Cathedral Square. Speakeasy aesthetic, European bar feel, live music every weekend, courtyard seating. The bartenders here are excellent. Perfect first stop of the evening before heading somewhere louder.

The Last Word New Regent St · 4 min walk

Intimate whisky and cocktail lounge on CHC's prettiest street. Over 600 whiskies on the shelf — staff know every one of them and offer guided tastings. Excellent cocktails and boutique wines too. Warm, cosy atmosphere. The right place to settle in for a few hours when it's raining and you don't want to move.

🍜 Asian — Korean & Japanese
Ramen Ria Riverside Market · 4 min walk

The closest restaurant to your hotel and the best ramen in Christchurch — noodles made fresh daily, bone broths brewed 8+ hours. The Pork Trio (tonkotsu with crispy pork belly, sliced pork, maple bacon, soft-boiled egg) is the signature. Tokyo-style ramen bar atmosphere inside the Riverside Market. Great for a rainy evening bowl.
